Decision01

First Price Wins

The first number a buyer hears sticks. Everything after compares to it.

28%less price drop
What it means

Buyers compare every later offer to the first one they heard. Setting the anchor high makes any concession feel small.

CRM Action

Track "first-price anchor" on every lead. Script agents to lead with the premium unit, not the discount.

Habit03

Surprise Builds Habit

Random, unexpected rewards beat predictable ones. The brain craves surprise.

67%more re-engagement
What it means

Slot machines work because rewards are unpredictable. WhatsApp CRM follow-ups should mix 5 message types randomly — not on a fixed schedule.

CRM Action

Replace fixed-interval scheduling with a variable-ratio engine. Property drops, market insights, tips — unpredictable sequence.

Unit 8 · Learning · Skinner

Memory04

First & Last Stick

Buyers remember the first and last thing. The middle is forgotten.

34%lift in close
What it means

The primacy-recency effect means property tours should show the best option first, runner-up last. Middle options barely register in memory.

CRM Action

Tour sequencer places highest-fit property in position 1 and 2nd-highest in the final position.

Unit 9 · Remembering & Forgetting

Context05

Situation > Personality

A tired buyer at 11pm is not the same as a fresh buyer at noon.

41%fewer frictions
What it means

The situation — time, mood, stress — matters more than personality. A "difficult" lead is often just an exhausted one. CRM logs context and adapts.

CRM Action

Tag every interaction with situational state. Auto-route late-night or stressed leads to gentle nurture.

Unit 7 · Sensation & Perception

Trust09

Trust Has a Shape

Some buyers need constant updates. Some need space. Some want direct asks.

47%nurture efficiency
What it means

Anxious buyers need micro-reassurance. Avoidant buyers need data-rich, low-pressure content. Secure buyers respond to direct CTAs. CRM auto-detects from interaction patterns.

CRM Action

Add attachment-style detection to intake. Auto-configure nurture cadence. Anxious=high touch. Avoidant=low touch. Secure=direct.

Culture10

Culture Decides

American, Gulf, and Singaporean buyers — three different decision architectures.

2.8xNRI conversion
What it means

US buyers want ROI and independence. GCC buyers want family validation. Singaporean buyers want legacy and growth. One pitch fails all three.

CRM Action

Cultural router: US → ROI-led. GCC → family-validated. SG → legacy-narrative. Geo-tagged auto-routing at ingestion.

Unit 26 · Cultural Psychology

Stress11

Stress Simplifies

Stressed buyers default to "no." Reduce stress or reduce choices.

38%faster close
What it means

Chronic stress impairs the prefrontal cortex — the brain's decision center. Voice stress analysis detects overload. CRM simplifies options for high-stress leads.

CRM Action

Voice stress score from calls. High-stress leads → only RERA-verified, ready-to-move inventory. Reduce choice, increase trust.

3.1xengagement
